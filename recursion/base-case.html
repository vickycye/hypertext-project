Base Case: What Remains Constant

Recursion stops here. Keep recursing through life experiences, but these constants remain true.

The Question

"If I strip away all the variables: the major, the career path, the location, the relationships, what remains? What are the base cases that define who I am, regardless of which function I call?"

The Constants

1. Curiosity

I need to understand how things work.

This will never go away, and I don't want it to. Every field I explore, every problem I encounter, there's always that spark of "but how?" This means I'll never be bored. There's always something new to learn, always another layer to uncover. This is a gift.

2. Care About Others' Experience

I want the things I build to make people's lives better.

Whether it's an interface, an explanation, or just a conversation. I genuinely care how others experience things. This means I'll never build something purely technical without thinking about the human on the other side. That's not a weakness; it's what makes the work matter.

3. Need for Meaning

I can't just do things because they're profitable or prestigious.

Some people see this as a burden, but I see it as a compass. This need for meaning will keep me from wasting years on work that doesn't matter. It's a filter that helps me say no to the wrong things so I can say yes to the right ones.

4. Creative Expression Drive

I need to make things, not just consume them.

From childhood drawings to code to design, the medium changes but the impulse stays the same. I'm a builder. That means every path I take will have opportunities to create. This isn't something I have to choose; it's something I get to bring with me wherever I go.

5. Growth Orientation

I'm never satisfied staying where I am.

After everything that's shaped me, I can't go back to unconscious living. This restlessness isn't a problem. It means I'll keep pushing, keep learning, keep becoming. Some people plateau. I won't. That's not arrogance; it's just who I am now.

What This Means for Futures

Any Path I Choose Must Include:

  • Opportunities to learn and explore
  • Ways to impact others positively
  • Connection to something meaningful
  • Creative outlets
  • Room to grow

The Liberating Realization

"If these are my constants, then maybe the specific path doesn't matter as much as I think. Maybe I can find these things in industry, or academia, or a startup, or creative work. Maybe the question isn't 'which path?' but 'how do I honor these constants in whatever path I choose?'"

The Scary Part

"But what if I'm wrong? What if these aren't really constants, just preferences I've convinced myself are fundamental? What if I change, and then I've built my whole identity on shifting sand?"

Reflection
