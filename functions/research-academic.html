The problems that matter, not just the problems that make money.

The Reality

  • 5-7 years PhD (low stipend)
  • Uncertain job market
  • Publish-or-perish pressure
  • Grant writing burden
  • Long path to stability

Am I smart enough for this? Am I committed enough?

I look at PhD students and they seem to live and breathe their research. Meanwhile, I'm not sure I can commit 5-7 years to one question. What if I'm just romanticizing intellectual work? What if my curiosity isn't deep enough, just wide? And even if I make it through—what then? Postdoc limbo? Adjunct poverty? Tenure-track jobs that don't exist?
