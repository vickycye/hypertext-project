who that was

slightly obsessed with video games

i came back from school and played hours of valorant, to where I can recognize a location on the map from a sound effect.

the joker?

i was always the "funny kid" in class, but looking back, I don't think I was the most respectful person.

comfort zone dweller

i sticked to things i liked. i sticked to things that were easy, and i stuck to things that were safe. but without risk-taking there would be no growth.

assumptions

i made a lot of assumptions about people and situations, and i wasn't the most accepting person back then.

the story of loss

after losing a loved one in the middle of my sophomore year of high school, I realized that I was living in a bubble. I was very focused on the current moment and didn't plan much for the future. I started connecting with my parents more, started reaching out to old friends from elementary/middle school, and started to pave a path for myself for the next 10 years.

what changed?

from gaming obsession to intentional play

i started to realize that I was using video games as an escape from reality, and I started to play them more intentionally. I started to play them with a purpose, and I started to play them with a plan.

from being goofy to being serious

i realized that I should become more serious about my work, how I present myself, and how I treat others.

from comfort to growth

i started to take risks, and i started to push myself out of my comfort zone by trying new things and challenging myself.

from assuming to accepting

i started to accept people for who they are, and i also started to accept myself for who I am in the moment without trying to shape myself into something I'm not.

what I still carry

i still have that same sense of humor, and I still love video games. but I'm also more aware of the time I'm spending on them, and I'm more intentional about how I'm using them.

the tension

often I think about the days back in high school, and how wonderful they were because I was so carefree and didn't have to worry about anything. but I also think about the days now, and how much I've grown and changed, and that's wonderful too.
